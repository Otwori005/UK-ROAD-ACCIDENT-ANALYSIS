# UK-ROAD-ACCIDENT-ANALYSIS
Data is the most powerful tool we have for public safety. I recently completed a comprehensive analysis of UK road accident data, transforming a dataset of 195,742 total casualties into a strategic safety roadmap.
My analysis identified three critical focus areas for UK stakeholders:

1. The Urban Risk Factor - Nearly 62% of all casualties occur in urban environments, suggesting that Vision Zero infrastructure and pedestrian-first urban planning are vital for local councils.

2. Infrastructure Vulnerability - Single-carriageway roads remain the most dangerous segments, requiring national highways to prioritize safety audits and better lighting in rural dark zones.

3. The Casualty Severity Gap - While total accidents sit at 144.4K, the presence of 2.9K fatal and 27K serious casualties underscores the need for high-impact enforcement during peak daylight hours, when 73.84% of incidents occur.

As a Data Analyst, I believe in creating visualizations that don’t just show what happened, but explain why it matters and how we can fix it. This dashboard serves as a decision-support tool for urban planners and emergency services. By mapping casualties by location with significant clusters in hubs like London, Birmingham, and Manchester, stakeholders can optimize resource allocation and golden hour response times. For policy influencers, the data suggests that focusing on Single Carriageway safety and Car-related incidents (155.8K) would yield the highest return on safety investments.
